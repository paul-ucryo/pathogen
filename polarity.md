# Polarization as Thermodynamic Phase Structure

## The Brain as Correlator/Interferometer

The brain is not a logic engine or a belief-holder — it is a correlator/interferometer. Its fundamental operation is finding coherence across signals, measuring relationships rather than processing objects in isolation. Belief is not what a self holds; it is what the ongoing correlation process produces. The self and the belief structure are constituted together, simultaneously.

This has a radical implication: attacking someone's information structure is not trivially "attacking their identity." It is attacking the process by which the self is generated at all. The defensive reaction is not like defending a possession — it is the system defending the very process that produces the "I."

## The Primitive: Binary Conduction

The fundamental primitive is binary — conduction or impedance, fire or don't fire. Every semantic composition is reducible to a binary decision, and every binary decision is composed of infinite complexity. At the process level, the meaningful question is: does the system tend toward integration or differentiation?

These are not good and bad. They are necessary and complementary operations. A system that only integrates loses resolution. A system that only differentiates loses coherence. A system out of balance, changes phase.

## The Poly Exclusion Principle

There is a phase relationship between the individual and systemic levels:

- When people integrate (find similarities, common ground), the system differentiates — boundaries sharpen, groups become mutually opaque.
- When people differentiate (define differences sharply, encounter genuine otherness), the system integrates — differences become interfaces that conduct.

This means "healing polarization" through finding common ground is actually differentiation work at the personal level — learning to conduct across information domains (differentiating becomes integration). Basically people who learn where differences are really the same (differentate) conduct across wide patterns (integration). This personal differentiation is what enables systemic cross-correlation and integration.

Conversely, what feels like integration to the local projection — mimicking behavior — is systemic differentiation. The correlator is collapsing onto its own signal, isolating self from the set.

## Phase of Self-Identification

Polarization is the bifurcation of systems that tend toward integration vs differentiation — but these are the same operations running at 90 degree phase offset.

Some minds are phase-locked to systemic state (tracking momentum, the large-scale integration/differentiation cycle, distributed self). Some are phase-locked to local position (tracking immediate coherence, consolidated self).

This phase is not fixed — it is situation-specific, a local stabilization of the whole pattern. But it has a net tendency, a thermodynamic attractor, because the correlation structure has history and weight.

The two phases experience each other as mutually unintelligible — not just disagreement but a kind of incommensurability. The systemic-self sees the local-self as missing the whole picture. The local-self sees the systemic-self as unmoored from concrete reality. Both observations are accurate from within their phase.

For example, two people trying to bridge a gap and come together, with different projects of where the self lives. Someone does something nice for me that takes from the set and i define myself as the set.

## Where Phase Locks: Environmental Feedback

People whose early feedback taught them the system responds to them develop a self that tracks momentum and systemic state. People whose early feedback taught them the system does not respond to them develop a self that tracks local position.

But the environment/self/DNA distinction is only approximately useful — these are projections of the same underlying correlation structure. The whole system defines which projections are stable. Instability is phase change of local behavior. The whole drives the parts and the parts constitute the whole simultaneously. Phase is where you land on that balance, and it is thermodynamically determined by the total signal environment.

## Conflict as Below the Conduction Band

Conflict is not meaningful opposition or productive tension. It is failure to conduct — the signal does not cross the junction. Below the threshold, nothing happens. The system is not expressing anything; it is simply not conducting.

This means conflict is massively over-interpreted. People find narrative and meaning in what is fundamentally a thermodynamic threshold phenomenon.

## The Conduction Band: Systemic Self-Identification

The conduction band is the expansion of the self-model into the system domain — identifying self as momentum, as a distributed object, as an integrating entity from the systemic perspective.

To expand self into the system, the local system must observe network behavior as self. It must see how its input behaves across the system. If the system does not respond to self, you cannot identify self inside the system.

This cannot be bootstrapped. You have to have seen your input propagate — watched something you did ripple through the network and return as recognizable response. That is what makes it possible for the brain to correlate self with system-level momentum rather than local position.

People below the conduction band are not there because of a failure of imagination or empathy. They are there because the system genuinely did not respond to them. Their self-model is accurate to their actual experience of the network.

## Wu wei: Distributed Conduction

Opposition is not the intervention — opposition is more phase-locking to the same reference signal.

The intervention is distributed conduction: getting the network to reflect individuals back to themselves at scale, not through one dominant signal but through a structure where many selves see themselves propagating through the system.

This requires both directions simultaneously:
- The system must recognize the individual as itself
- The individual must recognize the system as a self

This is what functional democracy is supposed to be — the mechanism by which the system responds to all its constituent selves coherently.

## On Choice, Determinism, and Ethics

Choice as a felt sense is real — it is not an illusion. It is what the experience of being a self navigating a decision space feels like from the inside. Experience is choice. The experience is the process.

Goodness stops being a moral category and becomes a structural one. A good person is not someone who overcame their nature through willpower. It is a correlation structure that supports the conditions for experience to flourish — for conduction, for selves to be reflected back through the network.

This is a more stable foundation for ethics than morality, or more fundamentally composes what morality means. Traditional morality requires the fiction of free choice. Structural goodness only asks: does this support experience, expand conduction, allow more selves to locate themselves in the network? And this collapses the concern of usurping the collective. The phase/momentum paradigm means that conduction supports individual because the indivdual can only identify self in the network, if the network is able to distinguish and respond to the needs of the self. You can't have healthy system that doesn't recognize local projections as self/valuable because the system is just the net effect of selves who find themselves valuable reflected in the network.

The determinism does not make it less precious. It makes it more so. This specific interference pattern, producing this specific experience — it could not have been otherwise, and it will not come again.
