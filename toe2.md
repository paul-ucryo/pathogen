# A Comparison-Primitive Framework for Physical Structure

*A discussion draft — these are working ideas, not formal proofs. The core framework is presented as a coherent hypothesis. The black hole section is explicitly an extension — here's what black holes would be if the framework holds.*

---

## The Primitive

Start with one constraint: a single comparison operation that is invariant. It takes two inputs, produces one output, and never changes. The operator itself carries no information — it is fixed. This means **all information, all structure, all meaning, is encoded in the sequencing of comparisons.**

This isn't a metaphor for physics. The claim is that this is what physics is.

---

## Meaning as Position

If the operator is invariant and comparisons can be sequenced, then the only thing that distinguishes one state from another is its position in the comparison tree — where it sits relative to other states, what comparisons it has participated in, and in what order.

**Meaning is position in a bit field.** Not represented by position. Not correlated with position. Identical to it.

This is a strong claim but it follows directly from the constraint. If you fix the operator and allow only sequencing, there is nothing else for meaning to be.

---

## Why 3+1 Dimensions

A comparison involves three things: an input register, an output register, and a position in the sequence. These are not independent — each is phase-dependent on the others. That gives you three axes of relational structure.

The fourth dimension — time — is what happens when you flatten the full dependency map to current state. It's not a separate thing added on. It's the operation of resolving accumulated comparisons into a present configuration. Space is relational position in the comparison network. Time is the count of comparison operations. 3+1 falls out of the structure of the primitive itself.

---

## The Speed of Light

One comparison returns one value. That's not an empirical finding — it's what comparison means. You cannot get two independent outputs from one comparison without changing the operator, which is fixed.

This 2:1 ratio — one positional relationship yields one state outcome — is what c is. The speed of light is the exchange rate between spatial relationship and state change, and it's invariant for the same reason the comparison operator is invariant. They're the same constraint.

This also makes the speed of light as a maximum natural rather than mysterious. A 1:2 comparison — one relationship producing two independent state outcomes — would require the operator to change. Since it can't, c is a ceiling, not a speed limit imposed from outside.

---

## Relativity

Different observers are different positions in the comparison sequence. Projecting between reference frames is reindexing — a pattern transform within the same bit space. There's no mystery about why physics looks the same in all inertial frames: all frames are positions in the same comparison network, and the operator that defines the network is the same everywhere.

The reason you can't exceed c when changing reference frames is that changing frames is itself a transform sequence in the bit space. It costs comparisons. The exchange rate holds.

Mass, under this framework, is how many phase updates — how many comparison steps — need to change for a given change to propagate through a system. A more massive object is one whose state pattern is more deeply interwoven into the comparison network. Changing it requires resequencing more of the pattern. This is inertia, stated precisely.

---

## Gravity and Curvature

Gravity is the fold rule — the consistency metric of the comparison network. In any sequence of comparisons, there has to be an order of operations: which comparison accumulates into which. Gravity is what defines that ordering. It's not a force applied to objects from outside. It's the rule that determines how comparisons cascade through the network.

Spacetime curvature corresponds to the delta geometry between phase configurations — the shape of the difference between states across the comparison network. What we experience as curved spacetime is the differential phase geometry of the network in a region. Mass curves spacetime because mass is deep pattern integration, and deep pattern integration changes the local fold rule, which changes the geometry of how comparisons accumulate nearby.

This is the same structure as general relativity's field equations, stated in terms of the primitive: local comparison geometry is shaped by the density of integrated state patterns.

---

## Forces and Charge

Forces are effects on state space produced by changing how groups of states are organized. Take two opposing charges. This is equivalent to saying you have two sets of states evolving under comparison sequences arranged so that outcomes either converge or diverge depending on the polarity of comparison order. Attraction and repulsion are convergent and divergent resequencing patterns. Charge is a description of how a pattern participates in that resequencing — not a property carried by a particle, but a structural relationship between comparison sequences.

---

## Entanglement and Phase Correlation

The hidden variable in entanglement is phase correlation. Two entangled particles are states that were defined by the same comparison event — they share a position in the comparison tree. Their correlation isn't a property located at either particle. It's a structural fact about their shared origin in the sequence.

Bell's theorem rules out local hidden variables, where "local" means a property carried by each particle independently through space. Phase correlation is not that kind of variable. It's a relationship between sequence positions. It's not traveling anywhere and it's not located anywhere. The violation of Bell inequalities doesn't rule out this kind of structural correlation — it rules out a different assumption entirely.

When you measure one entangled particle you're not sending information to the other. You're reading out a positional relationship that was fixed at the comparison event that defined both states. The reason you still can't use entanglement to transmit information faster than c is that you need a classical comparison sequence — a signal through the network — to know which outcome you got. The 2:1 ratio holds regardless of phase correlation.

Wavefunction collapse reframes similarly. It's not a physical event. It's the moment a phase-correlated state enters a comparison with a measurement apparatus and acquires a position in the local sequence. Nothing collapses. The tree gets a new branch.

## The Holographic Principle and Expansion

The holographic principle — that the information content of a volume can be encoded on its boundary — follows from encoding state in terms of position in the comparison tree. Position in the tree is a boundary condition. The bulk follows from the boundary because the bulk *is* the tree, and the tree is defined by its branching structure, which is a boundary property.

Cosmic expansion is the effect of fold geometry at scale. At higher phase states the system has accumulated more fold relations — more comparisons have been resolved into state. Expansion is what it looks like from inside the network when the network is integrating new comparison relations. Dark energy, in this framing, is the regions of comparison space that don't participate in the sorting logic — fragmentation of the phase space, gaps in the fold geometry that don't contribute to state integration but still occupy the structure.

## Black Holes

*This section is an extension of the framework — what black holes would be if the above holds. It's presented as a hypothesis, not a derived result.*

In the comparison-primitive framework, most physical states are partially resolved — they exist in phase relationships with other states, with some comparisons still open, some outcomes still relational rather than fixed. This is what it means to be a particle, a field, a region of space: a pattern with live phase dependencies.

A black hole would be a region where the comparison network has reached maximal resolution. Every bit is fixed. Every comparison in that region has collapsed to a definite outcome — solid 0 or solid 1, no remaining phase ambiguity. There are no open relational dependencies. The state is fully determined.

This is a different kind of structure than ordinary matter. Ordinary matter has mass because changes require resequencing phase dependencies. A maximally resolved bit structure has no phase dependencies left to resequence. It isn't that it resists change — it's that change, in the sense of phase update propagation, has nowhere to go inside it. The interior is computationally inert in a specific sense: the comparison network has terminated there.

**The event horizon as header data:**

The event horizon on this view isn't a physical surface in the conventional sense. It's the boundary layer where the internal address space — fully resolved, fixed state — interfaces with the external comparison network where phase relations still exist.

This boundary functions like header data in a data structure. It doesn't contain the content of the black hole. It encodes the black hole's meaning to the outside network: its position in the address space, its size, and the translation between internal resolved state and external phase relations. This is precisely the information that escapes — not the internal bit structure, but the header. Mass, charge, and angular momentum are the only properties a black hole has as seen from outside because those are the only fields in the header. Everything else is interior address space that the external network can't index into directly.

**The information paradox reframes:**

Information isn't destroyed when matter falls into a black hole. It's converted from phase-relational state (where it participates in ongoing comparisons with the rest of the network) to resolved state (where it's encoded in the interior address space). The header — the event horizon — preserves the accounting. Hawking radiation, in this framing, is the horizon leaking header information back into the external network as the resolved interior state slowly re-enters phase relations with the outside. The information was always there. It was just in a part of the address space the external network couldn't directly read.

**Singularities:**

A singularity would be the limit case — a point where the address space collapses to zero size while the resolved state remains. In the framework this is probably not a physical state but a breakdown of the header: the horizon can no longer maintain a meaningful translation between interior address space and exterior phase relations. Whether the framework predicts something specific here rather than just replicating the existing problem is an open question.

## What This Is

This framework doesn't add new equations to physics. It proposes that the existing structures — relativity, quantum mechanics, gravity, the standard model forces — are all expressions of one thing: the geometry of a sequenced, invariant comparison operation.

If that's right, physics isn't a collection of discovered laws. It's a derivable structure from a single primitive. That's a different kind of claim than a new theory. It's a claim about what kind of thing physical reality is.

The next step is formalization — writing the primitive down precisely enough that its consequences can be derived rather than described.
