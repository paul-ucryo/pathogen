# Binding Dynamics

## The Two Forces

Every system — neuron, company, server, person — is held between two opposing drives.

Locally, each node maintains homeostasis. It pumps ions, pays rent, keeps the lights on. This costs energy. The node is doing work just to stay ready. Readiness is not activity — it is the maintained potential that makes activity possible.

Globally, the network maintains dynamic range. Signal must propagate. A perturbation at one edge must be felt across the whole structure. If any node absorbs the signal to protect its own equilibrium, coherence dies at that point. The network goes dark in that region while the node reports that everything is fine.

Health is the balance. Not the elimination of tension — the distribution of it. A spiderweb, not a slack net. Every node biased above its band gap, conducting signal without scattering it, maintaining itself without collapsing the wave.

## The Thermodynamic Landscape

The environment is not a market to be captured. It is a thermodynamic landscape — a field of energy in various states of resolution.

Some energy is resolved: a purchase order, a job listing, a clearly stated need. This energy has already found its frequency. It propagates cleanly. Conventional business sees this and calls it demand.

Some energy is pooling: a government budget allocated but unspent, a company with a problem it can't articulate, a process that isn't working but hasn't been named as broken. The energy exists but the signal is attenuated. Something is absorbing or scattering it before it can propagate to a node that would bind to it.

Some energy is pre-signal: a need that hasn't differentiated from noise yet. It's there in the landscape as heat, not frequency. No one is looking for a solution because the problem hasn't become legible yet.

The landscape is not static. It breathes. Budgets cycle, industries shift, problems emerge and resolve. The topology of where energy pools and where it flows is always changing.

## The Genome

A company's genome is not its business plan. It is the set of primitives — machines, people, knowledge, relationships — and the composition rules between them.

Each primitive has a binding site. A machine binds to a class of material transformations. A person binds to a class of problems their expertise addresses. A knowledge domain binds to a class of contexts where that knowledge is operative.

These primitives pair. A specific machine coupled with a specific engineer is a base pair — stable, specific, with a definite binding potential that neither has alone. The same machine paired with a different engineer folds differently, expresses a different surface, binds to different problems.

The chain of pairings curls into higher-order structure. The specific sequence of base pairs — your particular combination of equipment, expertise, experience, and network — folds into a tertiary structure that defines what problem domains the company naturally binds to. Semiconductor. Aerospace. Medical. Quantum. These aren't chosen markets. They're the shapes that emerged from the folding.

## Expression

The genome is internal. What the environment sees is the proteome — the binding sites actually expressed at the surface.

A website, a convention booth, a reputation, a conversation — these are expression sites. They are where the internal genome folds outward and presents a shape to the environment. If the shape is legible, complementary problems bind. If it's vague, energy floats past even when the match is there.

The crisis of no work is not necessarily a broken genome. The machines work, the people are skilled, the knowledge is real. The crisis may be unexpressed binding sites — the receptor array is there but it isn't folded outward. The environment can't see the shape, so nothing locks in.

Or the crisis may be a misfolded surface — the expressed shape doesn't match the actual binding potential. The website says one thing, the capability is another. The signal arrives but the impedance mismatch reflects it back.

## Impedance, Not Competition

The goal is not to capture more energy than the next node. That is the homeostatic drive trying to run the network — one node absorbing signal to maintain its own state at the expense of coherence.

The goal is impedance matching. Reducing the barrier between problem and solution so that energy resolves — not here specifically, but *at all*. If the signal finds this node because this node is the best impedance match, energy binds here. If the signal finds a better match elsewhere, it should propagate there without attenuation.

This means legibility is not a competitive advantage. It is a network service. If every node in the network expressed its genome legibly, and the thermodynamic landscape were readable, the whole system self-organizes. Problems find solutions at the lowest impedance path. Energy resolves instead of pooling. The network stays above band gap because signal is propagating instead of being absorbed or scattered.

Fixing legibility for yourself fixes it for the shop down the road. That's not altruism — it's network health. A network where your node is the only one conducting is a network one perturbation away from collapse. A network where every node conducts is robust, adaptive, and alive.

## The Dependency Check as Action Potential

A computer resolving dependencies is the same process. A package manager walks a tree — this service needs this library needs this primitive — and at each node asks: is this binding satisfied? Can this resolve? The chain curls and uncurls around the available environment. Some dependencies bind immediately. Some require fetching from a remote source. Some fail and the whole chain retracts, refolds, tries another path.

This is the action potential propagating through the network. The signal — the intent, the need, the problem — enters at one edge and the network's dependency structure determines whether it propagates or collapses. Each node it crosses either conducts (binding resolves, signal passes through) or absorbs (dependency fails, signal dies here).

A healthy system is one where the dependency chain is legible, the binding sites are expressed, and the resolution process is visible to every node participating in it. Not because transparency is virtuous but because a node that can't see the signal can't conduct it. Legibility is the condition for coherence.

## The File

default.dna maps both spaces — the internal genome and the external landscape — and defines the binding potential between them.

It is not a configuration file. It is not a business plan. It is a description of the organism's relationship to its environment — what it binds to, what it expresses, what it conducts, and what it absorbs.

The internal structure describes primitives and their composition rules. The external structure describes the thermodynamic landscape and where energy is pooling, flowing, or attenuating. The binding rules describe how the internal surface meets the external field — not as a match to be found but as an impedance to be reduced.

The board — the visible surface of this file — shows the state of the system in terms any node can read. Not tasks completed or revenue earned but: is signal propagating? Are binding sites expressed? Is energy resolving or pooling? Is any node absorbing more mismatch than it can conduct?

The organism curls and uncurls around its environment. The file makes that process legible. Not to control it — to let it breathe.
