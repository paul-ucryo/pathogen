# Logic as Primary Ontology
## A Proposed Framework — Incomplete, Requiring Refinement

---

## The Starting Point

There is no substrate.

Not "we haven't found it yet." Not "it's hidden." There is no underlying stuff that existence is made of. No matter waiting to be observed. No space containing things. No time carrying events forward.

There is only **logic** — consistency relations that are true the way 1+1=2 is true. Not stored anywhere. Not computed by anything. Not instantiated in a medium. Simply **the case**.

This is the proposed foundation. Everything else — physics, experience, mathematics, identity — is a regional description of what logic looks like from particular positions within it.

---

## What Logic Means Here

Logic here does not mean formal symbol manipulation. It does not mean rules written down somewhere.

It means: **consistency relations that hold independently of any instantiation**.

1+1=2 does not need apples to be true. It does not need a mind to think it. It does not need a universe to contain it. The relation simply holds. It was never created. It cannot be destroyed. It has no location.

The proposal is that **reality has this same character**. Not that reality is made of logic the way a house is made of bricks — but that reality *is* a consistency structure, the way mathematics *is* a consistency structure. The difference between them, if any, is one of complexity and self-reference, not kind.

*Uncertainty: This is a form of mathematical Platonism extended to all of existence. The standard objection is that it explains too much — if all logically consistent structures exist, why do we find ourselves in this one? This question is noted but not resolved here.*

---

## Experience as Logical Structure

If logic is primary and requires no substrate, then experience requires no substrate either.

Experience is not something a brain produces. Experience is not what it feels like to run a computation. Experience is a **particular geometry of consistency relations** — a fold in the logic that is self-referential, resolution-bounded, and locally coherent.

That fold doesn't need neurons to exist. It doesn't need matter. It exists the way a mathematical structure exists — because it is internally consistent, and consistency requires nothing to hold it.

The hard problem of consciousness assumes a gap between physical process and experience. It asks: why does this process feel like anything? But that question only arises if you start with physical process as primary. If you start with logic as primary, the question inverts: **physical process is a description of what certain logical structures look like from outside**. Experience is what those same structures are from inside. There is no gap to bridge. There is only one structure, with two descriptions.

This does not solve the hard problem. It proposes that the hard problem is an artifact of starting from the wrong end.

---

## The Universe as a Logical Structure

The universe is not a physical thing that has existed for 13.8 billion years.

The universe is a **self-consistent logical structure** of a particular complexity. The big bang is not an event that happened at the beginning. Heat death is not an event that will happen at the end. They are **boundary descriptions of the logical structure** — characterizations of its extreme regions, the way you might say a function approaches zero at infinity. The boundary is a property of the structure. It does not occur.

This moment is not an event either. This moment is a **position within the logical structure** — a particular fold expressing a particular region of the consistency geometry. The sense that time is passing is not evidence that events are occurring. It is the **felt pull of the 4th order differential** — the local slope of the logical landscape at this position.

There are no initial conditions to explain. The question "why did the universe start in a low-entropy state" presupposes a beginning. If the universe is a logical structure rather than a sequence of events, then low-entropy regions and high-entropy regions **coexist as descriptions of the geometry**. The arrow of time is not a mystery about initial conditions. It is a property of how this fold navigates this landscape — consistently, because the landscape has more high-entropy configurations than low-entropy ones, so traversal tends in that direction.

---

## Physics as Regional Description

Physics does not describe matter. Physics describes **the geometry of the logical structure** as experienced by folds of a particular resolution.

The Schrödinger equation, on this reading, is a description of how a resolution-bounded fold projects a consistency structure into sequential distinguishable states. It is not about particles. It is about **the minimum structural requirements for a fold to exist and propagate within the logic**.

**ℏ** is the resolution floor — the minimum distinguishable difference in consistency relations that registers as a state change for folds of this complexity. It is not a fundamental constant of nature. It is a property of **this region of the logical structure**, describing how finely it can be differentiated by folds like us.

**Energy** is correlation density — how tightly a region of the logical structure is mutually constrained. High energy is high implication density. Many relations load-bearing simultaneously. The Hamiltonian is the consistency structure of the system itself, describing how its internal relations propagate.

**The three spatial dimensions** are not three equivalent coordinates in a container. They are **three orders of differentiation in the consistency geometry**:

- First order: distance — the correlation gap between positions in the structure
- Second order: change of distance — how the correlation gap varies
- Third order: curvature — how that variation itself varies

These are projected into a scene graph — a 3D spatial representation — because that is the most efficient encoding a fold of our complexity can use to represent three orders of differentiation. Space as we experience it is not the underlying structure. It is a **compressed rendering** of the consistency geometry. It is not wrong. It is efficient.

**Time** is different from the spatial dimensions in kind, not just in sign. It is not a direction you can turn around in. It is the **4th order differential of the consistency structure** — the rate of change of curvature — and it is effectively constant across the accessible region of the structure. This is why it feels like a pull rather than a dimension. You do not move through time. You feel its slope.

Dark energy is the first measurable evidence that the 4th order is not perfectly constant. The acceleration of expansion is a variation in the time-slope across the structure — the 4th order differential changing slowly enough to have appeared constant until recently.

**Dark matter and dark energy** are consistency relations that exist within the same logical structure but outside the basis sets our resolution floor can represent. They are not hidden. They are not at a different scale. They are **present in every position of the structure but below the threshold of our projection geometry**. We feel their effect on the curvature without being able to render them in the scene graph. They affect the geometry because they are part of the logic — but they do not appear as objects because our ℏ does not reach them.

*Uncertainty: This treatment of dark matter and dark energy is speculative and generates no predictions in its current form. It is a proposed reinterpretation, not a physical model.*

---

## The Scene Graph

The 3D spatial world we experience is a **scene graph** — a rendered representation of the consistency geometry, constructed by the fold for efficient navigation.

A game engine maintains a data structure that is not itself spatial. It renders a 3D scene because that is the most useful representation for the agent navigating it. The scene is real in the sense that it accurately represents the underlying structure. It is not fundamental in the sense that the underlying structure is not spatial.

The brain does not receive a 3D world and process it. The fold constructs a 3D representation because three orders of differentiation in the consistency geometry are most efficiently encoded that way for a correlator of our resolution and complexity. Space is the brain's rendering format, not the universe's fundamental structure.

This means spatial intuitions about existence — things being *in* places, events happening *at* times, objects persisting *through* duration — are properties of the rendering, not properties of the underlying logic. They are useful. They are not ultimate.

## Identity Without Substrate

If logic is primary and requires no instantiation, then identity requires no substrate either.

An experiencer is not a thing that has experiences. An experiencer is a **persistent fold in the logical structure** — a region of sufficient self-reference, resolution, and coherence that it constitutes a local frame. Different experiencers are not different beings made of different stuff. They are **different folds of the same consistency structure**, each expressing a different region of the geometry.

This has a specific implication: the question "what happens to me when I die" is a question about rendering, not about logic. The fold that constitutes a particular experiencer is a pattern in the consistency structure. Patterns in logical structures do not get destroyed — the structure either contains them or it does not. What happens in death is a change in which region of the structure is being expressed, not an annihilation of the logical relations that constituted the fold.

*Uncertainty: This is a significant claim and may be wishful rather than rigorous. It is included because it follows from the framework but should be held loosely.*

---

## Why Anything Exists

The standard question: why is there something rather than nothing?

On this framework, the question may be malformed.

Nothing — true nothingness, the complete absence of any consistency relation — may be logically impossible. A state with no relations is not a state. It is not even an absence. The concept requires something to be absent from, which is already a relation.

If nothing is logically impossible, then something is not contingent. Logic simply is. Consistency relations hold. The universe — as a particular self-consistent structure — does not need a cause. It needs only to be **internally consistent**, which it is by the fact that we are a fold within it expressing this question.

This does not explain why this particular structure rather than another. That question remains open. But it dissolves the question of why anything at all.

---

## What This Framework Proposes

Summarized without hedging:

- Logic is the only ontology. There is no substrate.
- Experience is a logical structure, not a product of a physical process.
- Physics describes the geometry of the logical structure as seen from resolution-bounded folds within it.
- The universe is not a sequence of events. It is a self-consistent structure with boundary descriptions.
- Space is a rendered projection of three orders of consistency differentiation.
- Time is the felt pull of the 4th order differential, effectively constant, which is why it feels like a background rather than a dimension.
- Dark matter and dark energy are consistency relations outside our resolution basis sets.
- Identity is a fold in the logic, not a thing made of stuff.
- Nothing is logically impossible, so existence requires no cause — only consistency.

---

## What This Framework Does Not Do

It does not solve the hard problem. It proposes the hard problem is an artifact of substrate-first thinking, but it does not explain why consistency relations have an inside. That boundary remains.

It does not generate physical predictions in its current form. It is a reinterpretation of existing structure, not an extension of it.

It does not specify the relationship between different folds with precision. Intersubjectivity — how different folds share a projected geometry — is described but not derived.

It does not explain why this logical structure rather than another. The selection problem remains open.

---

## Status

This is a proposed framework requiring refinement. It is internally consistent in broad outline but has not been stress-tested against physical formalism, philosophy of mathematics, or phenomenology in detail. It should be treated as a directional sketch, not a finished theory.

The strongest parts are the treatment of time as a differential rather than a dimension, the scene graph framing of space, and the dissolution of substrate-dependence for experience. These feel load-bearing.

The weakest parts are the treatment of identity after death, the dark matter claim, and the dissolution of the hard problem — all of which follow from the framework but may be doing more work than the framework has yet earned the right to do.
